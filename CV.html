The incidence of human mobility on the spread of the COVID-19: A Case study in Boston

University at Buffalo, Supervisor: JiYoung Park

• Examined the relationship between trends in the spread of COVID-19 and mobility near different types of amenities within the city of Boston by building a multiple linear regression model.
• Confirmed that retail stores and increased mobility near public transport hubs have a significant positive correlation with new daily COVID-19 cases.
• Provided data to support the rationale of stay-at-home and mask orders.

Full 2021

Changes in Motor Vehicle Thefts Patterns during the COVID-19 Epidemic in Buffalo, NY

University at Buffalo, Supervisor: Li Yin

• Explored the spatiotemporal changes in vehicle theft patterns in Buffalo, NY during the Covid-19 epidemic, by constructing a spatiotemporal analysis model based on STL; Moran's I, and MGWR.
• Identified the spatial shift in vehicle theft patterns during COVID-19 from downtown to surrounding disadvantaged neighborhoods as well as a shift in incident temporal patterns from nighttime to daytime.
• Defined the characteristics of communities in which vehicle theft frequently occurs, providing a reference for formulating urban management strategies in the post-epidemic era.
