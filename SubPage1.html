Investigating potential transferability of Chinese/Indian Cosmic Theory to an Academic Campus in the US UB South Campus

  • By: Yixuan Zhao
  • Date: May 17, 2021

The aim of the project is to use the principles of cosmic theories in China and India to develop a comprehensive, walkable, safe and active South campus for the University at Buffalo. This hypothetical proposal would also attempt to provide guidelines for active, inclusive and replicative campus planning strategies.

We have used the following methods and steps to arrive at our proposal/ theory of change:

  1. Analysis of similarities and differences between the Indian (Madurai) and Chinese (Beijing) cosmic theories through research on precedents. Both cities are studied in detail to understand the application of cosmic theory in different contexts.
  2. Narrowing down the list of overlapping features and predicting the outcomes of using each of those features.
  3. Understanding campus models for framing guidelines and good practices in American campuses.
  4. Study of the context of the UB South campus, precedents, and future proposals for expansion.
  5. Developing overlays with the Indian theory and the Chinese theory. This process involves testing out the planning principles on the layout of the South campus, elimination and addition of features and pathways, and use of urban design strategies.
  6. Figuring out points of conflict and harmony and research on similar precedents and campus models.
  7. Testing out the guidelines in the layout of the UB South campus.
